Vishrambaug Wada

ABOUT VISHRAMBAUG WADA

Vishrambaug Wada, located in Pune, is a historic building built in the early 19th century by the Peshwa Baji Rao II. The wada is renowned for its stunning architecture and historical significance. It was originally a residence and administrative center during the Peshwa era. The structure showcases a blend of Maratha and Mughal architectural styles, featuring intricately carved wooden pillars, large courtyards, and elegant balconies. The wada has been well-preserved and stands as a testament to Pune's rich cultural heritage. Today, Vishrambaug Wada serves as a popular historical landmark and a venue for various cultural events and exhibitions.
