Lal Mahal

ABOUT LAL MAHAL

Lal Mahal, located in Pune, is a historic palace that was originally built in 1630 by the Maratha king Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj's father, Shahaji Bhosale. The palace is renowned for its striking red walls, which gave it the name "Lal Mahal," meaning "Red Palace." It was the residence of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj during his childhood. The original structure was demolished in the 19th century, but a reconstruction was completed in 1988. Lal Mahal holds significant historical importance and is a popular tourist attraction, offering a glimpse into Maratha heritage and architecture. The palace is situated amidst bustling markets, making it a prominent landmark in Pune.
